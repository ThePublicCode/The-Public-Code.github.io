I started writing code at 13. A Toronto Star article about Ubisoft's Montreal studio, a copy of Visual Basic 6.0 from my dad's coworker, and that was it. I've been building things ever since.

Professionally that turned into 15 years — at Shopify, at Points International, leading teams, shipping systems, solving hard problems for organisations that had the budget to pay for it.

The Public Code is what I do with the same skills when the budget isn't there. Not as charity. As a deliberate choice — because the work some of these organisations are doing is too important to be blocked by a spreadsheet.
